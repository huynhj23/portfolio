Daemon Killer


Project Summary & Process

Daemon Killer is an Action Horror game that I made with a group of Computer Science students for my capstone project using Unity. The 3 of us all wanted to create a game that was fun and retro-like that showcases our capabilities in coding. We gathered together for a few meetings to decide the work that needs to be done, and divided the work among us that makes the most sense.

The project was a 4 month long endeavor where the team would meet up once week, or more if deemed necessary. The Project Coordinator, Brian Brookwell, a professor at MacEwan University who specializes in game development, would also be there, listening in on our discussion and provided feedback on our progress. Most of the time, he was satisfactory with our direction and pace of development, because it matches our projected milestone goals for the month.

Collaboration was the key to our success. There are often times where I had to coordinate with our level design artist to come up with new gameplay mechanics to make a level more interesting. As such, we both discussed a lot on the limitations and possible ideas we could implement such as targetting environmental objects. New puzzles became available and depth was added to the game.






















Gameplay Element - Lore

Large portions of the city is run-down and abandoned by the populace due to a surge of demons appearing out of no where. These creatures feast on humans, hunting them down whenever and wherever possible. The government has sent out a special division force where the humans are capable of magic, specifically, able to infuse their weapons with elemental powers to deal with these demons. A source of the demons has been determined to be inside an abandoned mansion far away from the city and the special division is tasked to eliminate the source of the demons.


Gameplay Element - Gameplay

The gameplay is in real time and uses an action gauge that fills up over time, which allows the player to act when it is full. When the gauge is full, the player enters targetting mode, where targetable objects are selectable and a list of actions will appear. Any amount of actions are permitted so long the player has enough energy to use all the actions. This allows the player for a power play when they are in need of a crutch. However, be wary because the energy expended exponentially increases as more actions are queued up.

The player is able to infuse their bullets with magic, allowing for special effects on the enemy. Examples consists of fire, electric, and force. Fire excels at destroying a single target. Electric is able to wipe a multiple enemies at once. Force pushes the enemy away. Players who hope to survive must think which element to use depending on the situation.

There are interactable objects scattered throughout the levels which add depth and strategy to the game. One interactable object is the oil puddle where it may be lit with the power of fire to explode nearby barrels. Be careful of the flames afterwards as it will hurt you if you cross its path.

Unique puzzles are everywhere in this game. Find your way around a room through the unique targetting system, allowing the player to see in the dark with the targetting circle. Solve a room by interacting with objects with the correct element. Use your wit and anything is possible
